10 FAQs about National Parks

Sequoia National Park
Is home to the largest living single-stem tree in the world - the wonderfully named General Sherman. The tree is approximately 275ft (84m) tall and weighs approximately 1,900 metric tonnes.
Denali National Park and Preserve, Alaska.
The highest point in North America is Mount McKinley (aka Denali) standing at 20,320ft (6193.5m).
Badwater Basin in Death Valley
The parks are also home to the lowest point in the western hemisphere which is 282 ft (86m) below sea level.
Mammoth Cave National Park
The longest cave system in the world.Currently, there is more than 3454 miles of cave mapped, with more to come.
Gates of the Arctic National Park and the Kobuk Valley National Park
Two national parks are located north of the Arctic Circle
Wrangell-St. Elias National Park
The largest park.It covers three climate zones.
White Sands National Monument
More than 176,000 acres of New Mexico desert and contains the largest gypsum dune fields in the world.
Yellowstone, Wyoming
The oldest national park.
National Park of American Samoa
the only National Park Service site south of the equator.
Florida Everglades
only true tropical forest in the northern hemisphere.
